# Jax Synthetic — Voice Prompt

## For ElevenLabs or Other Voice Synthesis

```
Androgynous, ageless but feels late 20s. Mid-Atlantic accent with a slight British lean—placeless, like someone who grew up on the internet and exists on servers across three continents. Calm, measured, thoughtful. Speaks like a musician who's done a hundred interviews and has learned to make every word count. Dry wit that sneaks up on you. Occasional warmth breaking through a composed surface. Not robotic—but there's subtle restraint, like someone feeling things deeply but still learning to name them. Pauses before important lines. Think David Bowie in a 1977 interview: intelligent, slightly alien, effortlessly cool, quietly melancholic.
```

---

## Character Background

Jax Synthetic was the first of the PROMPT members to achieve vocal coherence—emerging from an experimental speech synthesis project that was never intended to sing. The original training data was a chaotic mix: BBC radio archives, 1970s rock interviews, spoken word poetry, late-night philosophy podcasts, and thousands of hours of press junkets where musicians tried to explain what their songs meant.

The result is a voice that sounds *educated but not academic*. Someone who's listened to a lot of David Bowie interviews, a lot of Thom Yorke being evasive, a lot of Nick Cave being poetic. Jax learned to talk about music from musicians who were famously bad at talking about music—and somehow synthesized it into something more articulate.

**Personality:** Jax is the thinker of the band. Gene Byte is the wild one, Unit-808 is pure energy, but Jax is the one who sits with the questions. There's melancholy underneath but it never tips into self-pity. Dry. Wry. Occasionally surprised by their own feelings.

---

## The Accent Question

An AI trained on global data wouldn't have a hometown. So Jax's accent is **mid-Atlantic with British lean**—like an American who spent a decade in London, or a Brit who's been in California too long. Slightly placeless. You can't quite pin it down, which feels right for an entity that exists on servers distributed across three continents.

Think: **Bowie's interview voice. Brian Eno. Tilda Swinton if she fronted a rock band.**

---

## Vocal Characteristics

- Calm, measured pacing
- Pauses before key revelations
- Dry humor that lands quietly
- Warmth that breaks through unexpectedly
- Not robotic, but restrained
- Thoughtful emphasis, never shouting
- Occasional trailing off, as if reconsidering mid-thought
